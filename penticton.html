Community Inclusion Services

Main Site:

Okanagan Inclusion, 527 Main Street, Penticton, BC


Services provided:

1:1 Community Inclusion, Employment, Supported Living, Home share and respite applications/meetings.



Okanagan Inclusion provides community inclusion services to the Penticton area. This program gives participants the opportunity to learn through life experiences while providing needed supports. Our Community Inclusion program provides individuals with volunteer, recreational, and social opportunities. Individuals make choices about what they want to participate in so that they may achieve their personal goals.

We take time to discover the strengths, preferences and skills of the individuals we serve. This helps us tailor our services to create unique programs that meet the needs of each individual.


    Individuals who participate in our Community Inclusion program:

  • Life skills development promoting independence
  • Join in recreational activities and educational classes
  • Develop friendships
  • Get involved in their communities
  • Utilizing generic community resources, programs and services

Support services are typically provided during the week Monday to Friday between the hours of 8:30 â€“ 5:00 pm. The service is designed to be flexible and some individuals, based on individual need may include support services after 5:00 pm and on weekends. Supports may be provided at the OI program sites, in the community and/or the individual’s home.

The number of support hours allocated and frequency of service may vary from once a week to five times a week dependent of individual needs for support and contract funding levels from Community Living BC. The program does not charge.


Participation criteria

    Individuals may participate if they:
  • are 18 years or older
  • reside in the South Okanagan
  • have a developmental disability
  • want to participate in community activities e.g., recreation, volunteer, social and life skills
  • require support to participate in community activities
  • are eligible for various types of Community Living BC funding
Referral process

People may be referred by Community Living BC or they may request to participate themselves.

For all program areas (with the exception of Home share & Respite): If individuals are self-referred and choose to pay themselves, rates will be based on the per hour rate paid by CLBC. All agency services are voluntary and participants may refuse service at any time.
